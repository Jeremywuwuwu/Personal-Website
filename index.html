Finding my Identity

More about me and my journey of studying in America

As an underrepresented international student at the University of Washington, my 4 years of experience has shaped me into becoming the person I am today. I was first born in Hong Kong and lived there for 10 years under the British government. Despite living in Asia, Hong Kong provided me a Westernized concept of living and lifestyle. After lower school, I returned back to Taiwan alongside my family and finished my middle and high school degree at a prestigious European international school under the International Baccalaureate system.

Despite being a minority and marginalized from opportunities in a new environment, I have developed a profound understanding of cultural diversity. I seek to use my dynamic background to find my passion in Data Science, Business Management/Entrepreneurship, Project Management, Informatics, and other Administrative works. I am committed to working in different states, not only subdue to Seattle, Washington. This would be an opportunity for me to explore more parts of America, enjoy my hobby of traveling, and learn more about different culture within the US.

I lack work experience in the work industry as expected. Graduating on June 2023 with my Bachelor of Arts Degree, I hope to find a full time job that can sponsor me an H1-B visa whilst I work OPT for the first year. I will also become a part time student with only one class in Spring 2023, so I am seeking a Spring Internship that I can dedicate the majority of my time on and learn more about the working environment in the US and gain hands on experience with my future goals.
